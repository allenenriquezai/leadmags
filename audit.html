1. Web presence — your "website" isn't yours

Finding

The website link on Google Maps opens a page on cebudentalimplants.com. That's a big directory site — not your own website. You don't own that page. You can't change it.

Why this matters

  • A patient searching "dentist in Baguio" lands on a page full of other clinics. They can compare you to 20 others without leaving. Most of them pick someone else.
  • You can't add a booking button. You can't post deals. You can't collect patient emails.
  • Your neighbor ET3RNITY already has their own website with services, photos, and 24/7 booking. That's what patients expect now.

What your competitors are doing

  • ET3RNITY Dental (Mabini St, Baguio): Has their own site with a services list, gallery, and online booking — open 24/7
  • Modern Smiles (Manila, benchmark): Custom site with HMO page, team page, and online consultation booking — this is where the bar is heading
  • Elevate Dental (BGC, benchmark): Multi-branch site, premium design — what the best clinics now look like online

Fix

Get a real website. It should have a booking button, your services list, a before/after gallery, and your HMO accreditation.

2. Google reviews — 4 reviews · 5.0 rating

Finding

Your Google page shows 4 reviews at a perfect 5.0 rating.

Why this matters

  • 88% of people trust Google reviews as much as a personal recommendation from a friend. Most patients check Google before picking a dentist.
  • 4 reviews looks like a new or unknown clinic. The trust floor is 25+ reviews. Below that, most new patients skip you.
  • Your 5.0 rating is perfect. The problem is not quality — it's volume. Your neighbor ET3RNITY has 26 reviews and 4.9 stars. A patient choosing between you two will pick them, not because they're better, but because they look more trusted.

What your competitors are doing

  • ET3RNITY Dental (Baguio local): 26 reviews at 4.9★ — that's 6 times your count, and they're right down the street
  • Modern Smiles (Manila, benchmark): 80+ Google reviews — this is what an "established" clinic looks like online
  • The rule of thumb: Once you hit 25+ reviews, new patients stop second-guessing you

Fix

Send a short SMS or email after each visit asking for a Google review. Most clinics get 8 to 12 new reviews per month this way — for FREE.

4. No visible appointment reminders

Finding

From your public pages — website, Facebook, and Google listing — there is no sign that your clinic sends appointment reminders. This is about what patients can see from the outside, not your internal system.

Why this matters

  • In the Philippines, about 1 in 4 patients miss their appointment when no reminder is sent. Send a reminder 24 hours before, and that drops to 1 in 10.
  • For a clinic with 80 appointments per month, that's the difference between 8 no-shows and 20 no-shows. At ₱1,500 per visit, that's up to ₱18,000 a month you're leaving on the table.
  • Even if you already send reminders, patients can't see that. Showing it on your site or Facebook builds trust before they even book.

What your competitors are doing

  • Any clinic on Setmore (free tier): Every booking auto-sends an email confirmation and a 24-hour reminder — no staff time needed
  • Modern-era patient expectation: Patients who have been reminded before expect it. If your clinic is silent, it reads as disorganized
  • The win: This is one of the easiest gaps to close — and one of the fastest to see results from

Fix

Set up SMS or email reminders through your booking system. If you already have this running, make it visible on your website.
